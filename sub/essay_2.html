Up in the Clouds (or If Rainbows Were Only Black and White)
REPHAEL TORRENCE RAAGAS Part 2

They did terrible things to Drizzle and other rainbow-colored Skyfolk in the camp. They were starved and yelled at, and they were sprayed with black and white paint that hid their true colors. They were constantly told that who they are is unnatural against tradition, and that it was a choice--and they needed to make the right choice and change themselves.

Drizzle was scared. He heard that the people who didn't comply with the practices in Spray the Shade Away were punished severely. They were sometimes hurt or electrocuted with lighting, so he did what he was told in the camp.

He knew he was born this way--different in a sense. But it still didn't make sense to him; if being born different is the problem, why isn't there a conversion camp for left-handed Skyfolk?
Once Drizzle was let out of Spray the Shade Away, he was traumatized by what happened. A part of him knew that what the people in the camp did was wrong, and he questioned the things they were taught, like if being rainbow was a choice, when did his mom and dad choose to be black and white? He knew he was born this way--different in a sense. But it still didn't make sense to him; if being born different is the problem, why isn't there a conversion camp for left-handed Skyfolk?

He kept these thoughts to himself as he grew up. He put layers upon layers of disguises to hide his identity, and he managed to do a pretty good job hiding his tinted secret. Nobody else found out about it, even when he went to work at the rain factory. He thought it was better this way--fitting in with everyone. This was the way it should be, as far as Skytown society goes.

But one day, a new employee entered the factory. Blizzard was his name, and he seemed like a normal Skyfolk. He was nice, and soon they became close friends. On a pleasant rain-pumping afternoon at work, Blizzard suddenly took Drizzle by the hand and pulled him to a small corner behind the rain reservoir.

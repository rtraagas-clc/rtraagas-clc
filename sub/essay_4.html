Up in the Clouds (or If Rainbows Were Only Black and White)
REPHAEL TORRENCE RAAGAS Part 4

He never thought that there were so many like him. Sure, there were a few he met in Spray the Shade Away, but he never thought so many people were experiencing the same thing. And there weren't just rainbow-colored Skyfolk. There were pink-purple-and-blue Bifolk, pink-yellow-and-blue Panfolk, black-grey-white-and-purple Afolk and other wonderful assortments of multicolored folk. There were even some supportive black-and-white folk there. He felt something he never had before: genuine happiness.

A big sign said, "We Pride Ourselves with Rain, We Pride Ourselves with Rainbows" pertaining to the town motto "Home of the Original Rain." People were dancing and celebrating, holding up smaller signs such as "It is better to be hated for who you are than loved for who you are not," and "When did you choose to be black-and-white?" Everyone was peacefully mingling with each other, happily parading around without any disguises to hide their true colors.

He never thought that there were so many like him. He never thought so many people were experiencing the same thing.
All of a sudden, two gunshots were heard, and it seemed as if the entire Skytown Police Force surrounded the paraders. Everyone was forced to surrender to the cops. They were escorted to police buses and brought to the town jail, and were held there until the court trial, without any of them knowing why they were there.

The day of the trial came, and the Skyfolk from the Shade Parade were led to the city convention center since there were hundreds of them. The judge started to address them.

Apparently, a "concerned citizen" filed a lawsuit, suing all multicolored Skyfolk. The "concerned citizen" stated that their complexion went against the town traditions, and that those traditions should be followed. She also said in the complaint that as a responsible elder and a servant of the law, she had the responsibility to uphold the traditions stated, and recommended that all the rainbow people be painted permanently black and white, and that all the multicolored folks be separated from the "law-abiding, normal people of Skytown lest the virus that is this dreadful technicoloration be passed on to others."

The multicolored Skyfolk were shocked by this statement. They were arrested all of a sudden, and they were not even permitted to hire a lawyer. The court was silent for a few minutes, but then Blizzard stood up.
